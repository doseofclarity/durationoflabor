The 4 Stages of Labor (Pillitteri)

First Stage (Dilation)
Onset of true contractions → full cervical dilation (10 cm). Longest stage.
Second Stage (Expulsion)
Full dilation → birth of infant. Pushing phase.
Third Stage (Placental)
Birth of infant → delivery of placenta. Shortest stage.
Fourth Stage (Recovery)
First 1–4 hours postpartum; maternal stabilization.
⏱️ Total duration (nullipara): average 12–18 hours; multipara: 6–10 hours. Varies based on many factors.

Second Stage (Expulsion)

Begins with complete cervical dilation (10 cm) and ends with birth of the infant. Divided into passive phase (fetal descent) and active phase (maternal pushing).

Nullipara average duration: ~1 hour (up to 3 hours with epidural)
Multipara average duration: ~20 minutes (up to 2 hours with epidural)
Signs of second stage: Urge to push, perineal bulging, crowning, increased bloody show.
Prolonged second stage: Nullipara >3 hr (with epidural >4 hr); Multipara >2 hr (>3 hr with epidural) → risk of maternal exhaustion/fetal distress.
Nursing tip: Encourage position changes, avoid prolonged Valsalva, and support perineal integrity.

Third & Fourth Stages

🔹 Third Stage (Placental)
Duration: 5–30 minutes (average 10–15 min). Signs of separation: fundus becomes globular, cord lengthens, gush of blood.
Prolonged: >30 min → risk of hemorrhage.
🔹 Fourth Stage (Recovery/Immediate Postpartum)
Duration: 1–4 hours after placenta delivery. Focus: fundus firm, lochia rubra, vital signs stable, bladder empty, bonding.
⚠️ Critical: Most postpartum hemorrhages occur in fourth stage. Assess fundus every 15 min ×1 hour, then every 30 min.

Factors Affecting Labor Duration

  • Parity: Multiparas have shorter labors (pelvis stretched, cervix more compliant).
  • Fetal size & position: Macrosomia, occiput posterior, or asynclitism prolong labor.
  • Pelvis adequacy: Android/platypelloid → longer, often obstructed.
  • Uterine contractions: Hypotonic (weak/irregular) prolongs; hypertonic (too frequent) causes distress.
  • Maternal position & mobility: Upright/ambulation shortens first stage.
  • Epidural analgesia: May prolong second stage (loss of urge to push).
  • Psychosocial: Anxiety increases catecholamines → slows labor; support shortens duration.
  • Induction/augmentation: Oxytocin can shorten but may cause hyperstimulation.

Prolonged & Precipitous Labor

⏳ Prolonged Labor (Dystocia)
Definitions:
- Latent phase: >20 hr nullipara, >14 hr multipara
- Active phase arrest: no cervical change >4 hr despite adequate contractions
- Second stage prolonged: nullipara >3 hr, multipara >2 hr (or +1 hr with epidural)
Risks: maternal exhaustion, infection, uterine rupture, fetal distress.
⚡ Precipitous Labor
Total labor <3 hours from onset to delivery.
Risks: Lacerations, postpartum hemorrhage, fetal intracranial hemorrhage, neonatal trauma.
Management: Avoid pushing until ready, support perineum, prepare for rapid delivery.

Partograph & Labor Monitoring

Partograph – graphical tool to monitor cervical dilation, descent, contractions, FHR, and maternal vitals. Alert line (1 cm/hr) and action line (4 hrs to the right) help identify prolonged labor early.

  • Plot cervical dilation hourly → deviation to the right of alert line → increase surveillance.
  • Crossing action line → consider interventions (oxytocin, operative delivery).
  • Descent of fetal head (station) also recorded.
  • Contractions: frequency, duration, intensity (mild/moderate/strong).
  • Maternal parameters: BP, pulse, temperature, urine output.
WHO recommendation: Partograph use reduces prolonged labor and improves outcomes.

Mnemonics & Clinical Pearls

📌 Stages Mnemonic "LATE"
L – Latent (0–3 cm), A – Active (4–7 cm), T – Transition (8–10 cm), E – Expulsion (2nd stage).
⏱️ Timing Mnemonic "10-1-1" (average dilation rates)
Nullipara: 1 cm/hr in active phase; Multipara: 1.5 cm/hr; Transition ~1 cm/hr.
📊 Prolonged Labor Causes (3 P's)
Powers (inadequate contractions), Passenger (malposition, macrosomia), Passage (CPD).
🩸 Third Stage "C.A.L.M."
C – Contraction of uterus (fundus firm), A – Adherent placenta (separation signs), L – Lengthening of cord, M – Maternal blood gush.
